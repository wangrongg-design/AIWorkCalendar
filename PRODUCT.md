# Product

## Register

product

## Users

AI Work Calendar serves domestic small and medium businesses that need a low-friction way to collect daily work updates, plans, project context, risks, and management reports. Primary users are company administrators, department managers, and employees who open the product during daily work, usually under time pressure and with little patience for complex setup.

Company administrators need to create organizations, manage teams, maintain projects, check subscription status, and keep payment decisions simple. Department managers need a fast view of their department's reporting coverage, work risks, project movement, and period summaries. Employees need the fastest possible path from "what I did today" to a usable daily report.

## Product Purpose

The product exists to turn scattered work updates into an AI-readable work calendar. Success means an enterprise can start trialing quickly, employees can submit useful daily reports with minimal typing, managers can see missing reports and risks at a glance, and AI can generate reliable daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, and yearly summaries from real work data.

The core product promise is: let AI automatically understand team work.

## Brand Personality

Professional, clear, trustworthy. The tone should feel like a competent enterprise work tool, not a consumer promotion or decorative AI demo. Copy should be plain, specific, and action-oriented. The interface should feel calm enough for repeated daily use, while making risks and missing reports impossible to miss.

## Anti-references

- Consumer promotion pricing patterns: avoid `19.9`, discount-badge language, livestream or coupon energy.
- Traditional OA heaviness: avoid dense bureaucratic forms, overloaded navigation, and slow approval-style composition.
- Generic AI SaaS cliches: avoid purple-blue gradients, glassmorphism, large decorative orbs, vague "empower" copy, and effects that do not clarify work.
- Marketing-first product surfaces: logged-in pages must prioritize workflows and scanability, not hero sections or campaign layouts.
- Hidden enterprise complexity: avoid asking employees for enterprise codes at login, avoid pushing billing operations into mobile clients, and avoid exposing payment logic outside the backend.

## Design Principles

1. Daily work first. The primary surfaces should help users fill, review, and understand today's work before showing broad statistics.
2. AI is an operator, not decoration. AI modules must present conclusion, evidence or risk, and an available action.
3. Enterprise setup must stay lightweight. Trial, team creation, employee import, subscription status, and payment should be simple enough for non-technical administrators.
4. Permissions should match responsibility. Company admins manage teams, projects, subscriptions, and payments; managers analyze their scope; employees focus on their own reports.
5. Trust comes from visible state. Payment, subscription, report status, AI analysis progress, and data export flows must show clear status and recovery paths.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast and interaction states. Status must never rely on color alone; risk, warning, success, and missing-report states require text labels or icons. Mobile and desktop text must fit containers without overlap. Motion should be purposeful and respect reduced-motion preferences. Touch targets should stay at or above 44px on mobile clients.
